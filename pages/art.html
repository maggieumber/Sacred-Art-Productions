    • Andrea Castaneda

    MEET OUR MEMBERS

    Andrea Castaneda was born in Honduras in 1991 in the city of Tegucigalpa. She is a a muralist, fine artist and illustrator that started to be known as a muralist in the Roatan island. Her work focuses on the environment, endangered species, global awareness, and human rights. It spreads a pro-democracy message and advocates for peace and disarmament. Her native country of Honduras inspires her to paint the beauty of her natural surroundings and celebrate Honduran and world ecology and culture. She usually paints elder people that for her represent the Earth. Her works has been exhibited internationally and made murals in Mexico, Washington DC.

    Jihad Abdul Sabur

    MEET OUR MEMBERS

    Jihad Abdul Sabur was born and raised in Bronx, NY. She grew up in a Muslim household where it is forbidden to draw nature and/or people. Islamically it is because after one dies, Allah would ask the person to bring the being to life which one would fail, and then the person would be taken to hell. Instead, Jihad did a lot of textiles and abstract art growing up. It wasn’t until she was in high school and had to draw scenes in a book for her school project. After seeing how amazing the scene looked, she began to take her artwork seriously. One person that also inspired Jihad to pursue her dream as an artist is her high school art teacher Siti. She always helped her developed her ideas on paper creatively and seeing how he inspires other students like Jihad; inspired her to give that same inspiration. When it comes to her art, she always loves to promote beauty in the female figure, especially when it comes to African American women. She chooses African American women and women of color.
