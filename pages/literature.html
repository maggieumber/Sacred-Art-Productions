Noah Humphrey

Wakanda Forever

I never freeze in the dangers, the strangers
unseen and unheard, if I had a word
for the torrent of change this year has
brought, I ponder it all stacked with melanin
stacked black as the night sky
wrapped in intelligence, wrapped in elegance,
I am the living residence
Of a person that admired a hero
who fought his battles alone, who in the face
of danger became a symbol of freedom, of
power, of the royalty that's engrained in our DNA
Then I look back to my own power, the power
to access higher education
the potential of what I can amass in my area,
in my neighborhoods, in my life
when the world may call me a thug, punk,
gangsta, unworthy, uncrowned, denied, hopeless
It's because heros like him that aspires me
to be a scholar, provider, crowned in kindness,
a leader, aware, and understanding
I'll cross my arms and hold them to my chest,
Black Panther your soul lives on, be at peace
in your heavenly rest
-Knowa Know

Smaller Moves

Fan the fires to douse out generational fear
Clasp the back of my hand blessed with ahimsa
Lead the prayer, lead the marches
Confess your fears, your thoughts, we feel your
honest intention
Now bring to me your crocodile tears
Or the fire that sears
The generational wounds are reopening again
You can listen, you can cry, be angry, laugh
But I will never pray for my own downfall
My skin is an eternal marathon
That will outlast the marches
I will be protesting my entire life
Thousands were arrested to bring down four
You spoke of truth after another already fought it
4 years ago
It's popular to be in the right when it's a social
movement right?
Numbers talk, your actions stalk
The corner of a movement that long should be
heard
But we must continue to fight for better days
To burn bridges of hate
To operate under the systematic seams of woven
impressions on a different level
Small moves, big moves
You're moving, moving forward
Turn that into action: We got to keep painting the
other side of the fence
-Knowa Know

Water Vessel

Where are the tears?
Because my thoughts are like water
To disappear or evaporate on the sky
and I tried to dry my tears
with shaky understanding that flowed by unafraid
thoughts
I can only be contained through glass ceilings
called faith
And I save face by saying my ocean drowns
anyone with a worthy vessel
In this aquatic distant sombering world called a
river, I dive right through
The water isn't clear at times, too much blood,
pollution, less control, more restrictions
My mana cannot flow with unjust actions
The pool of thoughts cannot move as it is blocked
by a dam of self affliction
Water is my saving grace, my mine of liquid gold
sealed with in plastic and glass
-Knowa Know

Jack Brendan Miller

Independence Day

You wrest the shelter from my chest with blooming
knotted hands
putting my fist through the mirror above a motel bed
there is no fix for the schism cutting our leaving words
or was it shadowboxing tongues
in a mess of a bedroom,
or in rooms where what has been layed upon us

comes first from the clutching dark
like a widower or hope with no children/
no voice/no body/no borders we can discern

So we billow the waiting offered in
unloading midnight that stretches & licks
its spinning hands around our visions

peeled back like monstrous pelts.
You call me a psychomasochist burning
empty words that pump the vitals from our
sleeping bodies, they say:
take my fruits/all violently re-arranged/take
from me/cut from the dark a secret seed
in violent ciphers that spring the life I move
with to find and cover a dancing slow
scalding--take these smudged young embers,
and see through prisms the leaving/
breaking/staying/shatter their glowing
mirror/

smear it on your windows while I
stretch on your bed/reflection/
in pieces of home.
-Jack Brendan Miller

Vita Rhie Quintanilla

MEET OUR MEMBERS

Vita Rhie Quintanilla is the author of The Schizophrenic Dialogues, Writer and Frontwoman of Salvation Day and cofounder of Sacred Art Produtions.

The Schizophrenic Dialogues is a collection of memoirs on the first-hand experience of schizophrenia told through the lens of the actual dialogue between a person with schizophrenia and her auditory, visual, and tactile hallucinations. These accounts and their accompanying commentary represent the reality of people with schizophrenia in society and within themselves. The Dialogues shed light on the lives built to harness a stigmatized condition as a positive, and live in defiance of a "grave" prognosis.

Crystal Garcia

The Glorious Dark

I see you up there!
Peeking from beyond the clouds
you hide behind...
not knowing if you should show
your full face to let your light shine.

Slowly, you emerge fully
out into the dark night sky.
It's ironic you bring me such delight
yet engender a yearning deep within me!
I want to hold onto your beauty;
the brightness you've shown me is
the only reason
I still have stars in my eyes.
The darkness I've seen constantly makes me
doubt my own light.

it's a strange comfort to know you're always
there,
but I feel you may be lonely all the awy up
there...
Stuck in a perpetual dance around us
as our own personal satellite--
however it could be you're a shadow artist
and the Earth is simply your muse.
Going through phases only to see
the various ways we look from afar
as you shine your reflected light
a little differently each night!
-Crystal Garcia

Misunderstanding

Misunderstanding seems to be
a way of life.
At least for the human race
as we destroy our home and
each other at a break-neck pace.
Humans living with hardly
any "humanity"...
wondering why we can't
seem to get ahead when
we only look behind...
Even when we look outside
towards the others,
we all realize we can barely survive.
All of our hearts drop at the realization
that we've been promoting violence,
condoning hate, and even loving strife.
-Crystal Garcia

Transcendence

The urge to want to feel beyond ordinary is
innately embedded in all of us...
A faint and common vibration gently shakes
our heartstrings, giving us the illusion of
feeling EVERYTHING--then maybe
nothing.

What a tricky thing to exist, to feel, even...
Our perspectives largely determine what we
experience in this sensate world
as events and emotions all blur together to
form our chaotic everyday lives,
living everyday yet somehow still struggling
to genuinely feel alive.
-Crystal Garcia

A Thunderous Hope

I remember being young
thinking I was just like
a hurricane--even wrote a
poem likening myself to the
infamous storm.

What I didn't realize in my naive
youth is I was not so much like
the storm as
I am the eye of the storm.
I am not merely the chaos I feel
surrounded by;
I am also the peace I know is
deep within me.

I cannot always see the blue
sky when it is overcast,
yet I can always focus being
more the eye of my own storm
as I perpetuate heavy showers.

Perhaps I am just trying
to cleanse this soul
from all the toxicity
it used to hold so dear.
-Crystal Garcia

Actually Through Broken Shades

We aren't exactly wearing
rose colored glasses--
perhaps it is more of an
individual preference.

These days we are starting to
see more clearly
despite smoke from the fires.
Buildings of the cities and
towering trees
of the forests both ablaze!

The haze settles in as riots
begin again.

We aren't exactly sure how to
process the atrocities
we witness from pictures and
clips off the internet to the
violence happening outside
& simultaneously within.
-Crystal Garcia

It seems in dreams,
